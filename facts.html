Christmas Facts

Christmas is a holiday celebrated by millions of people around the world. It is a time of joy, love, and celebration, and it is also a time to learn about the rich history and traditions of this beloved holiday. From the origins of the Christmas tree and the legend of Santa Claus, to the customs and traditions practiced in different countries, there is so much to learn and discover about Christmas. Here are some fascinating facts about this holiday that you may not have known

The first recorded use of the phrase "Merry Christmas" was in 1534.

The tradition of hanging mistletoe dates back to ancient Norse mythology, where it was believed to possess magical powers.

The song "Jingle Bells" was actually written for Thanksgiving, not Christmas.

The famous Christmas song "Rudolph the Red-Nosed Reindeer" was written in 1939 by Robert L. May as a promotional giveaway for the Montgomery Ward department store.

The popular Christmas plant poinsettias are native to Mexico and were first introduced to the United States by Joel Roberts Poinsett, the first U.S. Minister to Mexico, in the 1820s.

The world's largest Christmas stocking measured 106 feet and 9 inches long and 49 feet and 1 inch wide. It was made by the Children's Museum of Indianapolis in Indiana, USA in 2007.

The first artificial Christmas tree was made in Germany in the early 19th century from goose feathers that were dyed green.

The world's largest Christmas tree was a 221-foot Douglas fir erected in 1950 in the town of Woodinville, Washington.

In the United States, the most popular Christmas movie of all time is "It's a Wonderful Life," which was released in 1946.

The original version of "A Christmas Carol," written by Charles Dickens in 1843, was only 7000 words long.

The first commercial Christmas card was produced in London in 1843 by Henry Cole, who was also the creator of the Victoria and Albert Museum.

The largest gingerbread house on record was built in Texas in 2013 and measured 60 feet by 42 feet. It used over 4,000 pounds of icing and over 2,000 pounds of candy.

The famous Christmas carol "Hark! The Herald Angels Sing" was originally called "Hark! How All the Welkin Rings."

The first Christmas stamp was issued in the United States in 1962. It featured a picture of the Nativity scene.

The first known reference to the tradition of kissing under the mistletoe appears in a Norse myth about the god Baldur.

The first known Christmas parade was held in Philadelphia in 1821.

In the United Kingdom, it is traditional to leave a mince pie and a glass of sherry for Santa Claus on Christmas Eve.

In Italy, it is traditional to serve a dish called "la befana" on Epiphany Eve, which is a sweet pasta shaped like a woman's face.

In the United States, the most popular Christmas gift of all time was the Rubik's Cube, which was released in 1980.

The largest Christmas stocking on record was 27 feet long and 16 feet wide, and was made by the Children's Museum of Indianapolis in 2007.
